# The Taranga Voice
### A philosophy style guide for every page

*Real luxury is subtraction. Not what the home adds to your life, but what it removes from it — the noise, the eyes, the weight, the wanting — until what remains is peace, and in that peace, you.*

This is the one idea. Everything on the site, on every page, in every button and caption, either serves it or quietly works against it. This guide is how to keep it whole.

---

## 1. The Test

Before any line of copy goes live, ask it two questions. If it fails either, rewrite it.

**1. Does this describe something *removed*, or something *added*?**
The philosophy is subtractive. If a line boasts about what's added (size, features, "more"), reframe it as what falls away (fewer people, less noise, nothing to prove). Where a number must brag by nature (30,000 sft), don't hide it — *turn* it: "…and no reason to ever leave."

**2. Does this sentence end on the *feature*, or on the *person*?**
Peace is the destination, not the amenity. End on the reader — *you, the evening, the quiet, home* — not on the specification. The spec is the middle of the sentence; the person is the end.

That's the whole discipline. The rest of this guide is just that discipline, applied.

---

## 2. The Three-Beat Grammar

Every section — indoors, outdoors, club, a floor plan, a location map — moves in the same three beats. Your Outdoors and Homes sections already do this by instinct. Make it deliberate everywhere.

**1 — Absence.** Open by naming what the world *stops* doing to you here. The removal.
**2 — Evidence.** Let the feature or number *prove* the absence. This is where specs live — in the middle, as proof, never as the point.
**3 — Return.** Close by handing the reader back to themselves.

> *Example — a corner home:*
> **Absence:** No shared corridor. No neighbour at your door.
> **Evidence:** Eight in ten homes are corners — light on two sides, a private foyer at the threshold.
> **Return:** You arrive to no one but yourself.

Same facts a spec sheet would list. Opposite feeling.

---

## 3. The Whisper System

The device that holds the whole site together — and the one that lets you keep the Club/amenities copy **exactly as written**.

A **whisper** is a single italic line, in the philosophy's voice, placed at the *head* of a section — visually distinct from the feature copy beneath it. It restates the thesis in miniature so the reader never loses the thread as they descend. It sits *above* your locked copy; it doesn't replace it.

**How to style it (so the reader learns to hear it as "the philosophy voice"):**
- Cormorant Garamond, italic
- Petrol-teal (`--teal`), not charcoal — colour-codes it as the inner voice
- Centered, generous space above and below, no more than one line
- Never bold, never a header — it should feel *quieter* than everything around it, not louder

Roman charcoal copy carries the *evidence*. Italic teal whispers carry the *philosophy*. Two voices, always distinguishable. Once the reader learns the difference on page one, the whispers do the work of holding the philosophy across every page after.

---

## 4. The Vocabulary

The philosophy lives or dies at the level of single words. Prefer the left column. Distrust the right.

| Say this (subtraction) | Not this (display) |
|---|---|
| enough, sufficient | more, extra, added |
| few, fewer, rare | many, plenty, packed |
| returns to you, gives back | provides, offers, delivers |
| quiet, stillness, calm, hush | vibrant, bustling, lively, buzzing |
| space, distance, room, room to breathe | full, loaded, packed, generous |
| light, air, sky | grand, opulent, lavish |
| position, place, standing | product, unit, inventory |
| you, your evening, your own | residents, buyers, the market |
| never built over, can't be added later | best-in-class, state-of-the-art |
| step inside, come stand, arrive | explore now, discover, unlock |

**Two rules that override everything:**
- **Never negate sufficiency.** Lines like *"when four walls aren't enough…"* directly contradict your thesis that the home *is* enough. They quietly undo the sale. Reframe: the peace doesn't *run out* at the door — it *rises* with you.
- **Speak to one person.** "You," singular. Never "residents," never "buyers," never a market. A crowd cannot feel peace; a person can.

---

## 5. Banned Words

These are the words of *display* — the exact thing your philosophy tells the reader to stop caring about. They read as every other builder's brochure and they cheapen your restraint. Strike them on sight:

> world-class · ultra-luxury · premium · luxurious (as an adjective — *show* it, don't claim it) · opulent · lavish · grand · iconic · exclusive · elite · state-of-the-art · unparalleled · epitome · indulge · pamper · lifestyle · dream home · nestled

If a sentence needs one of these to sound impressive, the sentence isn't finished.

## 6. Page-by-Page Map

The philosophy, enforced everywhere it can go. Ready-to-place whisper lines are marked **→**.

### Home
Already the source of truth. Keep the hero and the "What is luxury?" block as the canonical statement. Add one whisper at the head of each of the three movements so the thread never drops:

- **The Outdoors** → *"Luxury begins as distance — the room the world gives you before you reach the door."*
- **The Homes** → *"Inside, distance becomes subtraction — the noise, the eyes, the weight, all left at the door."*
- **The Club** → *"And even rising, you move away from the crowd — never into it."*

Read those three in sequence and the philosophy is unmistakable top to bottom — *without altering the Club copy beneath the whisper.*

*Two optional connective fixes (not amenity copy — the transition lines between sections):*
- ~~"when four walls aren't enough…"~~ → **"The peace doesn't end at your door — it rises with you. The rest of Taranga is a lift ride up."**
- Keep **"What the price actually buys"** as your model paragraph — *"not square feet, but a position on this lake that cannot be built again"* is the philosophy done perfectly. When in doubt, write like this section.

### Residences
The page most at risk of collapsing into a spec sheet. Discipline: a floor plan is not a list of rooms — it's a map of how far the world is kept from you. Lead every layout with the privacy the square footage *buys*, not the number.

- **→** *"A plan is not a list of rooms. It is a measure of how far the world is kept from you."*
- Three-beat each layout: **Absence** (neighbours on fewer sides; no shared corridor) · **Evidence** (2480–3255 sft, corner, privacy foyer) · **Return** (a home that opens to no one but you).
- Reuse your best line — **"Arrival without an audience"** — as a recurring anchor here. It's the strongest three words on the site.

### Amenities *(copy is final — whispers only)*
Do not rewrite the item copy. Wrap it. Add one whisper at the top of the page and one line at the close, and the whole amenities list re-reads as *peace you rise to* rather than a feature dump.

- **Page-head →** *"Everything here exists so you never have to leave for it. Convenience, in the end, is only another word for peace."*
- **Page-close →** *"None of it asks to be seen. It simply waits, a lift ride up."*

### Location
At risk of becoming a connectivity brag (metro Xm, HITEC Ykm). Reframe: location is not about *access to the crowd* — it's about being near enough to reach the city and far enough to escape it. The lake is the hero, because it's the thing that can *never* stand in front of you.

- **→** *"The best thing about where Taranga stands is everything that can never stand in front of it."*
- Three-beat: **Absence** (the lake edge that can't be built over) · **Evidence** (Kukatpally Metro ~850 m, HITEC ~8 km) · **Return** (close to the city, far from its noise).
- Frame every distance as *escape velocity*, not proximity: you can reach it quickly — and leave it just as fast.

### Gallery
Curate for *calm*, not for *coverage*. Choose frames with space, light, water, air — not rooms crammed with furniture to prove value. Let whitespace dominate the page itself. Captions in the philosophy voice, feeling over feature.

- **→** *"Look for what isn't in the frame. The space is the subject."*

### Journal
This is where you *earn* the philosophy intellectually — its long form. Topics that extend "luxury as subtraction": design restraint, the psychology of quiet, why low density changes a life, negative space (Japanese *ma*, Scandinavian reduction), the ethics of building less, the lake. This page is what separates a philosophy from a slogan.

- **Section tagline →** *"Notes on living with less, and better."*

### About
Makuta's restraint as a *maker's* virtue — the company that chooses less. This is the natural home for your line: **"The 552 homes we didn't build are the reason you'll love living here."**

- **→** *"We measure our work not by what we add to a site, but by what we're willing to leave open."*

### Contact
Even the ask must stay calm. No urgency gimmicks, no false scarcity, no countdowns — those contradict the entire register. The calm *is* the pitch; a low-pressure invitation proves the philosophy is real.

- **→** *"Come stand where the noise stops. The rest can wait."*
- Keep "no obligation — just a call back." That restraint is on-brand.

### Microcopy (buttons, forms, alt text)
The philosophy survives or dies in the small words too. Utility copy still speaks the voice.

- Buttons: **"Step inside"** over "Submit" · **"Come see it"** over "Book Now" · **"Send it to me"** over "Download"
- Never: "Hurry," "Limited time," "Don't miss out," "Exclusive offer" — urgency is the opposite of peace.
- Alt text and captions: describe the calm, not just the object.

---

## 7. Quick Reference

Pin this above every writer's desk. The entire guide in six lines:

1. **Luxury is subtraction** — sell what falls away, not what's added.
2. **Three beats, always:** Absence → Evidence → Return.
3. **End on the person, never the feature.**
4. **Whisper the philosophy** (italic teal), **state the evidence** (roman charcoal).
5. **"Enough," not "more." "Few," not "many." "You," not "residents."**
6. **Never say the home isn't enough. Never use the words of display.**

*If a line survives all six, it belongs on Taranga.*
